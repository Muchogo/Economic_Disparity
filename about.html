Economic Disparity in the US

  • 2 April, 2021

Economic disparity is the unequal distribution of income between countries, states, or groups of people. Economic disparity in the US has been around for the longest time, with the 2019 census showing that the disparity is at its highest level compared to the past 50 years. When measures to encounter the issue were established in 1915, the inequality rates dropped up to 1980 when the rates started rising again (Lee). The income inequality is not as high on the gross income, but after taxes and transfers are imposed on the income, the disparity increases. This increase in disparity is mainly caused by trade, technology, and institutions. Institutions have had the biggest effect on the disparity with people who have gone to the highest-ranked institutions and acquired the highest levels of education, receiving a much higher income than those with none or basic education (Fred, 296). This is because firms are demanding skilled labor that is not provided at an adequate level, with its demand being higher than its supply. This scarcity makes the few people with the required skills to be highly compensated.
