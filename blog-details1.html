Position 2

  • 2 April, 2021

Income disparity, just like any other issue facing the economy, has both a good and bad side to it. The pros of income disparity include; the trickle-down effect, which means that an increase in the income of the wealthy people in the country leads to an increase in the income of those lower than them in the income levels as the wealthy will invest the increased income that will benefit the others through job opportunities in those investments (Berg, Borensztein). Another pro is a reward for hard work, risk, and investment. Disparity ensures that those people who work the hardest in the economy get just as much compensation, and those that risk their money in investments are highly rewarded for that risk as things could have gone wrong. They could have lost their invested capital. However, the cons for the economic disparity are the strongest and have the biggest effect on the social and economic state of a country.

Disparity ensures that those people who work the hardest in the economy get just as much compensation, and those that risk their money in investments are highly rewarded for that risk

This disparity creates social problems that involve riots and increased crime rates as those with lower income try to get some extra money for their needs. These crime rates are also fuelled by the low-income earners' anger from the injustices they experience in terms of the income they get. The disparity also increases the country's rate of poverty because the country's GDP increases following the high increase in the rates of wealth by the high-income earners. An increase in the poverty level affects the country's economic state, whereby it will have most of its citizens not in a position to cater to their basic needs. The cons of economic disparity outweigh the pros in terms of the effects they have. Therefore, it is right to conclude that economic disparity is a bad thing.
